Understanding Nutrition Basics

Nutrients are substances your body needs to function properly. They're divided into macronutrients (needed in large amounts) and micronutrients (needed in smaller amounts but equally important).

üçû Macronutrients

What they are: Carbohydrates, proteins, and fats that provide energy and building blocks for your body.

Daily needs: Measured in grams

Primary function: Energy production and tissue building

üíä Micronutrients

What they are: Vitamins and minerals needed in small amounts for proper body function.

Daily needs: Measured in milligrams or micrograms

Primary function: Metabolic processes and disease prevention

Macronutrients: Your Body's Main Fuel

üçû Carbohydrates ‚ñº

Function: Primary energy source for your brain and muscles

Types: Simple (sugars) and Complex (starches, fiber)

Recommended: 45-65% of daily calories

Best Sources:

Whole grains Fruits Vegetables Legumes

Why you need them: Carbohydrates are broken down into glucose, which fuels cellular processes. Complex carbs provide sustained energy and fiber for digestive health.

ü•© Proteins ‚ñº

Function: Building and repairing tissues, making enzymes and hormones

Components: Made of amino acids (9 essential)

Recommended: 10-35% of daily calories (0.8g/kg body weight)

Best Sources:

Lean meats Fish Eggs Beans Nuts

Complete vs. Incomplete: Animal proteins contain all essential amino acids. Plant proteins often need to be combined to provide complete amino acid profiles.

ü•ë Fats ‚ñº

Function: Energy storage, hormone production, nutrient absorption

Types: Saturated, unsaturated, trans fats

Recommended: 20-35% of daily calories

Healthy Sources:

Olive oil Avocados Nuts Fatty fish

Essential fatty acids: Omega-3 and omega-6 fatty acids must be obtained from food as your body cannot produce them.

Creating a Balanced Diet

MyPlate Guidelines

The USDA MyPlate model recommends filling half your plate with fruits and vegetables, one quarter with whole grains, and one quarter with lean protein, plus dairy.

üçé Fruits & Vegetables

Goal: 5-9 servings daily

Benefits: Vitamins, minerals, fiber, antioxidants

Variety is Key:

Dark leafy greens Colorful berries Citrus fruits Cruciferous vegetables

üåæ Whole Grains

Goal: At least half of grains should be whole

Benefits: B vitamins, fiber, sustained energy

Choose:

Brown rice Quinoa Oats Whole wheat

üêü Lean Proteins

Goal: 5-6.5 oz equivalents daily

Variety: Mix animal and plant proteins

Options:

Fish & seafood Poultry Beans & lentils Nuts & seeds

ü•õ Dairy & Alternatives

Goal: 3 cups daily

Benefits: Calcium, protein, vitamin D

Choose:

Low-fat milk Yogurt Cheese Fortified alternatives
